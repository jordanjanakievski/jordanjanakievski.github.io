Start With Why

Simon Sinek

Being a leader is not easy. Public speaking, positivity, and enthusiasm have never been my strong suit. But finding a message and finding a purpose for doing what I do is easy. I always know what I am doing and why I do it. Sinek tried to have us focus on our message and practice implementing it into our lives. We want to live our best lives possible. I found that if people resonate with my message, the struggles I face become easier because those people will help me improve myself. A "why" is not something that is done alone, but requires collaboration and support from others.

Don't Sweat the Small Stuff...and It's All Small Stuff

Richard Carlson

Learning to focus on the important aspects of my life was very beneficial. All I knew was to have a completely packed schedule and to never stop working hard. This book was able to help reflect on certain parts of my day to day that meant more than otehrs. I learned that there was a lot of energy wasted on tiny, minisule events that served no purpose on making myself a better person, leader, and friend. The moment I started to let little problems not bother me was the moment I freed myself from the routineness of student life.
